Stephen Hawking

The Theory of Everything

  • 1942 - January 8 in Oxford, England on the 300th anniversay of the death of Galileo
  • 1959 - Began studying physics at University College, Oxford at the age of 17 despite his father wanting him to study medicine and follow in his footsteps. He also became coxswain of the row team during his time there.
  • 1962 - Stephen began studying cosmology at the University of Cambridge under the supervision of Dennis William Sciama, one of the founders of modern cosmology
  • 1963 - Multiple events
    • He meets his first wife, Jane Wilde, at a New Year's party
    • He is diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS aka Lou Gehrig's Disease) and given a diagnosis of 2 years to live
  • 1968 - Becomes a member of the Institute of Astronomy in Cambridge
  • 1969 - His disease continues to progress and he becomes confined to a wheelchair
  • 1973 - Publishes his first book, The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time, with G.F.R. Ellis
  • 1974 - Multiple events
    • Reaches celebrity status in the scientific community when he discovers what is now referred to as Hawkins Radiation
    • Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society becoming, at the time, one of the youngest scientists to become a Fellow
  • 1979 - Named the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge University, a position that dates back to 1663
  • 1985 - He comes down with pneumonia threatening his life. The tracheotomy that was performed took away his ability to speak
  • 1988 - He publishes A Brief History of Time, a short book designed to explain the complexities of cosmology to the average person. It spends 40 weeks at the top fo the London Sunday Times best-seller list
  • 1990 - Stephen separates from his wife Jane and moves in with one of his nurses, Elaine Mason
  • 1995 - He divorces Jane and marries Elaine
  • 2001 - He writes The Universe in a Nutshell, a follow-up of A Brief History of Time which was a more illustrative guide the cosmology's big theories
  • 2003 - Police reports are filed indicating that Stephen was being abused by his wife Elaine. Stephen denies the accusations so the police close the case.
  • 2005 - Another follow-up, A Briefer History of Time, is published to make it more understandable
  • 2006 - Stephen divorces Elaine
  • 2007 - Multiple events
    • He writes a science book for children, George's Secret Key to the Universe, with his daughter Lucy
    • Stephen gets to experience weightlessness while traveling in the special zero-G Boeing 727 for more than 2 hours over the Atlantic
  • 2010 - He publishes The Grand Design which speaks against the idea that God created the universe
  • 2011 - He participates in a trial for the iBrain, a device that can be worn that reads the wearer's thoughts by processing the brain's electical signals
  • 2014 - Multiple events
    • The movie The Theory of Everything is released in theaters depicting the early life of Stephen Hawking
    • Stephen spoke out about the dangers of artificial intelligence, or AI, and the possible consequences of it
  • 2015 - He holds a news conference in London to announce the launch of the Breakthrough Listen project, a project devoted to the discovery of extraterrestrial life
  • 2016 - He hosts a new show called Genius on PBS in May

Stephen has also played a number of cameos in television shows and movies over the years.
